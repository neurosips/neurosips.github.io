EEG Atlas

We have gathered the following EEG Atlas for the adult and child neurology residents and fellows. The following collection is mainly based on the “must-know” EEG findings by Nascimento et al with some modifications.

Normal EEG

  • Awake, adult PDR
  • Awake, pediatric PDR
  • Drowsiness, slowing of PDR
  • Stage 2 sleep, sleep spindles
  • Stage 2 sleep, K complexes
  • Drowsiness, diffuse irregular delta-theta slowing of background
  • Stage 1/2 sleep, vertex waves
  • Drowsiness, slow roving lateral eye movements
  • Stage 1/2 sleep, POSTS
  • Stage 3 sleep, diffuse irregular delta slowing
  • Rapid eye movement (REM) sleep, erratic eye movements
  • Rapid eye movement (REM) sleep, sawtooth waves

    Normal variants EEG

  • wicket spike/rhythm
  • Hyperventilation-induced slowing
  • benign epileptiform transients of sleep
  • Breach rhythm
  • Rhythmic mid-temporal theta of drowsiness
  • Hypnopompic hypersynchrony
  • Hypnagogic hypersynchrony
  • Photic driving
  • Mu rhythm
  • Posterior slow waves of youth
  • Photomyogenic response
  • Lambda waves
  • Temporal slowing of the elderly
  • 6 Hz spike-and-wave bursts
  • Frontal arousal rhythm
  • 14-and-6 Hz positive spikes
  • Increased frontal beta activity
  • Subclinical rhythmic electrographic discharge in adults
  • Midline central theta (Ciganek rhythm)
  • Occipital needle-like spikes of blindness
  • Slow fused transient
  • Fronto-central rhythm (texting rhythm)
  • Frontal sleep spindles
  • Alpha variant (fast/slow)

  • Eye blinks
  • Electrode pop

  • Eye fluttering
  • Lateral eye movements

  • Lateral rectus spikes
  • Chewing/bruxism
  • Sweat
  • Glossokinetic
  • Nystagmus
  • Respiration/breathing

  • Generalized epileptiform discharge,3 Hz
  • Generalized epileptiform discharge, 3-5 Hz, polyspike
  • Focal seizure
  • Focal epileptiform discharge, spike
  • Focal epileptiform discharge, sharp
  • Generalized seizure, absence
  • Generalized epileptiform discharge, <2.5 Hz
  • Focal epileptiform polyspike
  • Generalized seizure, tonic
  • Infantile spasm
  • Hypsarrhythmia
  • Continuous and intermittent focal polymorphic slowing
  • Intermittent rhythmic delta activity
  • Generalized polymorphic slowing
  • Generalized rhythmic delta activity
  • Diffuse regular/rhythmic slowing
  • Abnormal PDR, slow for age
  • Abnormal PDR, asymmetric
  • Asymmetric sleep spindles
  • Photoparoxysmal response
  • Centro-Temporal (Rolandic) spikes
  • Generalized paroxysmal fast activity
